Payment Options

Fee payment can be made using Visa, Master Card, cheque or money order. Cheques and money orders are payable to the University of Toronto.

Fees are due on the first day of a course.

A 5% reduction will be made for fees paid in advance, that is before the first day of a course.

A 5% premium will be charged for fees billed to a third party.

Fees for 12 week courses can be made in three equal monthly installments due on the first day, on the first day of the fifth week and on the first day of the ninth week of the course.

Fees cover all materials and resources for the course. There are no additional charges.

To withdraw from a course with a full refund, you must withdraw by the end of first day of a course, unless you are enrolled in a twelve week course. To withdraw from a twelve week course with a full refund, you must withdraw by the end of the fifth day of the course. Later withdrawals will be assessed on a pro-rated basis.

Fees paid are tax deductible, a Tuition and Education Amounts Certificate will be issued after successful completion of a course.

Funding Resources

Employment Insurance recipients should contact an Employment Ontario counsellor regarding possible funding, before applying to any of our courses.

OSAP assistance may be available to those who qualify. This assistance is only available for 12 week courses. Please see the OSAP web site for more details. Apply to OSAP, early, as applications can take 6-8 weeks to process. When completing the OSAP application, the name of this course is DEEDS Project, the course load is 100% (full-time), and the course duration is 12 weeks. If for any reason you do not take the course you can cancel your OSAP application without penalty. Please be aware that you will not receive ANY money from OSAP before the course actually starts.

As a result of recent changes to legislation, recent immigrants who have lived in Ontario for less than twelve months may be eligible for an OSAP loan. The twelve month residency rule for immigrants may be waived on appeal of the original decision. That is you must apply for an OSAP loan. If your application is denied you can then appeal this decision.

This course qualifies under the Lifelong Learning Plan. The Lifelong Learning Plan is a Government of Canada initiative which allows qualifying students access to their RRSP funds for the payment of course fees. For more information see your 1999 or later income tax return guide or check with Canada Customs and Revenue.
