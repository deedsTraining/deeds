Course Topics

  • Overview of the Android Platform.
  • Setting up the Eclipse Development Environment and Integrating the Android SDK.
  • Resources in XML.
  • Content Providers.
  • Introduction to SQLite.
  • Intents.
  • Designing User Interfaces for Android using XML and Java.
  • Common Controls.
  • Adapters.
  • Layouts.
  • Menus.
  • Dialogs.
  • 2D Graphics.
  • Touch Screen Programming.
  • Sensors.

Course Description:

  • Our courses are held at the University of Toronto, St. George campus (downtown Toronto)
  • This course will include lectures and hands-on supervised computer labs, split 50% lectures and 50% labs.
  • Students will be able to access their work remotely at any time.

Course Requirements:

  • Prerequisites: Advanced Java, XML, SQL, and Unix/Linux
  • Seats are limited. Course registration is on a first-come, first-served basis.

Tuition Fees:

  • The tuition fees for this course have not yet been decided.
  • Tuition Fee payments are by Certified Cheque or Money Order payable to the University of Toronto.
  • A deposit of $500 is required to hold your place in the course.
  • Full payment of the remaining fees is due on the first day of the course.
  • A 5% premium will be charged for fees billed to a third party.
  • Fees cover all materials and resources for the course. There are no additional charges.
  • To withdraw from the course with a full refund, you must withdraw before the end of the first lecture of the course.
  • For later withdrawals a refund will be assessed on a pro-rated basis. Materials already provided are not returnable for credit.
  • Fees are tax deductible, a Tuition and Education Amounts Certificate will be issued after successful completion of this course.

Funding Resources:

  • Employment Insurance recipients should contact an Employment Ontario counsellor regarding possible funding, before applying to any of our courses.
  • This course qualifies under the Lifelong Learning Plan, if you are eligible. The Lifelong Learning Plan is a Government of Canada initiative which allows qualifying students access to their RRSP funds for the payment of course fees. For more information see your 1999 or later income tax return guide or check with Canada Customs and Revenue.
  • This course DOES NOT qualify for OSAP.
